<p style="font-size: 12px; color: #666; font-style: italic;">
A histopathological whole slide image (WSI) is a digital scan of a 
microscope slide containing stained tissue, used by pathologists to 
detect disease such as cancer. WSIs enhance accessibility, support 
accurate diagnosis, and drive advances in computational pathology. 
Common stains like Hematoxylin (purple/blue nuclei), Eosin (pink 
cytoplasm and matrix), and DAB (brown biomarker highlights) enable 
precise detection of cellular and molecular targets.
</p>
